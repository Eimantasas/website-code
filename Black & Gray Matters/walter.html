Heisenberg found dead in a meth lab

Walter Hartwell “Walt” White Sr., also known by
his pseudonym and business moniker Heisenberg is an
American former chemist and major narcotics distributor
from Albuquerque, New Mexico, whose drug empire became
the largest meth operation in American history, surpassing
both Gustavo Fring’s drug empire and the Cartel’s. Before
entering the drug trade, Walt was a respected chemist and
scientist who later worked as an overqualified high school
chemistry teacher at J. P. Wynne High School alongside working
at the A1A Car Wash to financially support his family. However,
later Walt started manufacturing chemically pure crystal
methamphetamine to provide for his family.

Walt went into hiding in New Hampshire and remained
in hiding for almost a year. Eventually, Walt returned
to Albuquerque for unknown reasons and was now found dead
in a methamphetamine laboratory with multiple others dead in
another building. The cause of these deaths seems to be a
machine gun contraption built in to a trunk of a car.

It was able to completely pierce through the walls of the
building and brutally murder everyone in it. The casualties
are devastating, but there seems to be evidence that at least
2 people survived and got away. Unfortunately, Walter Hartwell
White was not one of those people.
